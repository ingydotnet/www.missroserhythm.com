Ukulele Lessons
You're never too young or too old to play the ukulele! This remarkably versatile instrument is easy to learn and brings great joy wherever it goes. If you are in the Seattle area and would like to take ukulele lessons from Miss Rose please drop us a line here. Miss Rose loves to teach beginners of all ages and will also design lessons for more experienced players who are interested in learning performance technique and "jazz" chords.

Ukulele Workshops & Classes
Miss Rose also offers workshops for music stores, festivals and ukulele clubs with exciting titles like "Ukulele for Absolute Beginners" (separate workshops for kids and grown-ups), "Let's Go to the Bar: Fun with Bar Chords", "Music Theory for Uke-sters", "Strum Fun for Ukulele", and "Performance Technique for Uke-Singers".

Beginning Ukulele
The ukulele is experiencing a new rise in popularity. Don’t you wish you too could play this extremely versatile & portable instrument? Learn to play in a supportive and fun group environment. This class will take you from an absolute beginner to someone who is ready to share music with your friends and family. We’ll explore chords, scales, strumming, singing, repertoire and all around musicianship. Bring your ukulele tuned G-C-E-A. We'll showcase what we've learned at a class party.

Ensemble Ukulele
In this class, students will learn different parts of the same songs and then play them together as an ensemble. Singing, strumming, picking & chord melody will all be explored. We'll work through a few select songs. It’s about quality, not quantity. Parts will be available for advanced beginners on up; students should be comfortable singing & strumming while changing chords smoothly. Please bring your uke tuned GCEA. Low G is recommended, but not required. Baritones are welcome, but please let instructor know in advance. We'll showcase what we've learned at a class party.

Ukulele Makers
Looking for a beautiful handcrafted ukulele? These folks build them and play them, and they are true works of art:
Earnest Instruments. Joel Eckhaus (Ukulele Eck) makes these ukes in Portland, Maine. He also makes mandolins & tenor guitars.
Mya-Moe Ukuleles. Gordon & Char Mayer make instruments that look & sound gorgeous and play beautifully. Be prepared to fall in love.
DaSilva Ukulele Company. Michael DaSilva makes his ukuleles in Berkeley, California, where he also hosts concerts and jam sessions.
Small Wonder Banjos. Jere Canote customs builds banjos, banjo-ukes, banjo-mandos, banjo-guitars and all things banjo right here in Seattle.
